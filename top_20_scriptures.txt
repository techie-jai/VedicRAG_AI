  category: Vedanta / Philosophy
  description: The Upanishads represent the culminating philosophical, mystical, and spiritual core of the Vedas. Composed largely between 800 and 200 BCE, they mark a monumental shift away from external fire sacrifices toward deep inward contemplation and self-realization. There are over 100 known Upanishads, but about 10 to 13 are considered the "Principal" Upanishads.   description: The Bhagavad Gita is arguably the most famous and universally revered of all Hindu scriptures. It is a 700-verse text forming a crucial section of the epic Mahabharata. Arjuna is paralyzed by a moral crisis, and Krishna uses this moment to impart profound spiritual wisdom. The Gita addresses Dharma (righteous duty), urging selfless action (Nishkama Karma), and details the four paths of Yoga: Karma (action), Bhakti (devotion), Jnana (knowledge), and Raja (meditation).
